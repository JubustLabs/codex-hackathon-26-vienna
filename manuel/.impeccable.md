## Design Context

### Users
Converge is for mixed decision rooms where domain experts and technical implementers need to align on how to build a feature. The immediate hackathon use case is a live session between a support lead and an engineering lead deciding how to build a customer service agent. The interface is used in real-time, often in a meeting or while recording a product demo, so comprehension needs to be immediate.

### Brand Personality
Calm, premium, intelligent. The product should feel like a quiet command room for high-stakes decisions, not a noisy AI dashboard. It should evoke clarity, trust, and forward motion.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dark-first, editorial, and cinematic. The center lane is the truth surface where conflict turns into common ground. Side lanes should feel quieter and more private, with visible evidence fragments that explain each position. Avoid cyberpunk, generic SaaS gradients, glassmorphism overload, and stock dashboard aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. The product should explain itself in the first frame.
2. The shared room is the hero; private lanes support it.
3. Show the basis for disagreement, not just the disagreement itself.
4. Make convergence feel earned through structure and motion.
5. Keep the UI human-first and avoid exposing internal system jargon.
